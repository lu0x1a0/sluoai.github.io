Project Description:

Develop a feasible solution that could extend existing functionalities within the field of education by utilizing Softbank Robotic's Pepper Robot.

Empathize

We conducted an initial brainstorming of areas in education that could utilize human robot interactions to enhance the experience. ultimately we pin pointed down to 3 key fields of sexual education, language & culture tutor and teacher training.

Speed Dating

For each ideas we created an storyboard for showcase in speeddating. During Speeddating we found that people still prefer to have genuine Human-Human Interactions and robots do not yet have the capability to replace human aspects of interaction. i.e. user can still differentiate between robot and human. So instead of having robot replaciung human functionalities, it is better to have robots to combine and enhance experiences of exististing human interactions or in fields which human are unwilling to have human interaction.

It was also surprising that people users was more in favour for the privacy gained by sex ed robot compared to the enhancement of features in other proposed solutions

Prototype

From the responses For storyboard we prototyped with pepper and later conducted user interviews. Code Sematics for three proposed use case scenarios are attached below.

  • Pepper engages in interactive conversation regarding consent.

  • Pepper presents biological videos/ demonstrations upon user request.

  • Pepper conducting private QA session with students to remove the awkwardness and fear for privacy for students to ask questions.

Verify

After receiving user responses for the prototype, we identified the following aspects that could be improved.

  • Need more physical demonstrations regarding biological learning
  • Subtle wording is preferred. Originally we took the raw video from UK's NHS and the phrasing was considered too frank in Asia regions, as well as for mass educational purposes.
  • Interaction methods should consist of more human aspects familiar to user for comfortable and seamless interactions, while maintaining its identity as a robot to allow comfortable questioning and non-intrusivity.
  • During QA and small learning modules, it should ask for user feedback for engagement.
  • More user customization os preferred. i.e. we should offer a range of voice for different gender. Perhaos the phrasing could also be learnt for according to gender and culture.

This projects brought me insights into the limitations of intelligent robots may be something that we can exploit. i.e. A robot doesn't need to completely replicate human behaviours and being limited to unable to conduct judgement can make users comfortable a lot of context, especially for data collection and educational purposes.
