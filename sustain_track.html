Sustainable Tracking Prototype

CS4461 Human Computer Interaction Project 1

Campaign: Sustainable Tracking

Team members:

  • Lawrence Tam
  • Jim Lin
  • Shreyas Agnihotri
  • Andreas Dachsberger

Project Description:

This is a project to initialise a new sustainable campaign within the HKUST campus that is engaging for targeting users through technologically interactive methods.

Empathize

Initially, our team identified existing sustainability campaigns on campus to gain inspiration upon possible campaigns. We have identified many through the mind map attached below:

Campaigns we identified includes:

  • reward for low energy consumption (in some halls)
  • adding a price for A/C usage
  • additional costs for takeout packaging
  • “say no to disposables” campaign (e.g. straws)
  • display on water fountains
  • various posters (recycling etc.)
  • air hand dryers instead of paper towels

Interpret

POV

After several interviews, we've identified the common issues that HKUST students have with most sustainable campaigns are:

  • Lack of time
  • Most don't pocess an ample budget
  • lack of feedback on own sustainability

Ideate

Various Solution Proposals

By using brainstorming tools like Metaphoraction, we came up with 5 different campaign proposals:

  • Virtual Endangered pet for Energy Saving
  • Virtual Forest for Proactive Recylcing
  • Visual feedback from bins to build recycling habits (Simple feedback)
  • FeedBack from Bins to Educate About Importance of Recycling
  • Personal Assistant to help building sustainable habits

Verify

StoryBoarding

We then drafted the interaction method between the user and campaign.
  • Virtual Endangered pet for Energy Saving

    • The user can view their electricity usage on a display installed in UG halls. The visual cue of a dying polar bear urges students to consume less electricity.

  • Virtual Forest for Proactive Recylcing

    • This campaign promotes the collaborate effort from student across the entire campus to recycle by growing a forest from sampling with the rate of carbon footprint reduction.

  • Visual feedback from bins to build recycling habits (Simple feedback)

    • As the user drop something into the bins, the object recogniser detects whether its thrown into the correct bin and provide an simple, direct visual feedback.

  • FeedBack from Bins to Educate About Importance of Recycling

    • Whenever the user dispose something into the bin. Base on the material, and whether its placed into the correct bin. The holographic interface educates by showing the corresponding destination of such waste.

  • Personal Assistant to help building sustainable habits

    • Users will be able to track their carbon footprint of their usage of different appliances on campus by voluntarily scanning QR codes

Speed Date

After several rounds of speed dating, we picked the 5th option as its preference rank was more consistent compare to other proposals. Upon the design of a paper prototype, we found that we lacked engaging methods of interactions in this proposal as scanning QR code constantly is - boring. We then considered different gestures that could simplify the experience of the app and maintaining engagement degree
